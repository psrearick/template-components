1

Discuss

I tailor each website I develop to my client’s specific requirements. For the service to be most effective, I need a clear picture of your company's values, goals, and needs. We begin the process with dialogue about your business. We will discuss your current marketing strategy - what’s working and what isn’t. That way, we can better understand the role of a new website in the marketing plan.

By the end of this consultation, you should come away with the information you need to decide if a new site would help you market your services. I would go away knowing enough to develop a plan for creating a website to meet your needs.

2

Contract

Once we have decided to move forward, I will prepare a contract to protect us both. Based on our previous discussion, it will include the scope of work and timeline. The agreement will also cover the specific payment terms, intellectual property ownership, and dispute resolution. The contract will define the change order process to define what happens in the scope of work that would need to be changed after the contract is signed. The contract is a critical piece of the process. By carefully reviewing and agreeing upon the contract terms, we can both be confident in the successful completion of the project. Once we both have signed the contract, we can move on to the next step.

3

Invoice

Once the contract is signed, I will send you an invoice for the amount we agreed to in the contract. This amount is usually fifty percent of the price for the completed website. The remaining portion and any additional fees, such as for hosting, will be paid upon project completion.

4

Plan

We will then discuss the website's design more in-depth and collaboratively than the initial consultation. We will discuss the plan for each web page, including text, images, and overall design. We will end this conversation with a clear understanding of the style and content of the website. If any assets are required, such as images, color palettes, logos, or copy, I will request them now.

5

Design

While you gather the required assets, I will work on an outline of the website design based on our previous conversations. It will thoroughly cover each aspect of the site. I will send you the design for approval.

6

Code

Once the design is approved and you have provided all required assets, I will write the code for the website, implementing the agreed-upon plan. This part of the process can have a relatively wide duration range, depending on the complexity of the project. The development process could be a day or several weeks. I will update you on its progress periodically, regardless of how long it takes.

7

Review

As we approach the finish line, I will make the website available as a public demo for you to review. If you disapprove of the result, we can make minor modifications as allowed in the contract. Significant changes will require additional agreements. The need for significant changes is a relatively uncommon occurrence.

Once you approve the site, I will send you an invoice for the remaining amount you still need to pay. Additionally, I will send a recurring invoice if you choose me to host your website.

8

Launch

It’s finally time for the site to go live. I will move the website to the agreed-upon hosting provider with the correct domain name.
